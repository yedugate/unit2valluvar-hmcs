1. Introduction

  • Food preservation = the science of preventing food spoilage by controlling microorganisms, enzymes, insects, and chemical changes.
  • Goal: Extend shelf life, maintain safety, retain nutritional value & sensory quality (taste, texture, aroma).
  • Principles used: Temperature control, chemical treatment, radiation, and packaging methods.

2. Preservation by Low Temperature

Refrigeration (0â€“5 Â°C)

  • Slows microbial growth & enzyme action.
  • Used for short-term storage.
  • Examples: Milk, fruits, vegetables, meat.

Freezing (â€“18 Â°C)

  • Stops microbial activity; enzymes become inactive.
  • Maintains food for months.
  • Examples: Meat, ice cream, bread.

Advantages: Maintains freshness, nutrition. Limitations: Cold chain required; some texture changes after thawing.

3. Preservation by High Temperature

Blanching (90â€“100 Â°C, short time)

  • Destroys enzymes in vegetables.
  • Pre-treatment before freezing/canning.

Pasteurization (60â€“80 Â°C, few seconds/minutes)

Sterilization (>100 Â°C)

  • Example: Canned soups, UHT milk.

Advantages: Safe, long shelf life. Limitations: Loss of heat-sensitive vitamins (C, B1), change in taste.

Q1. What are the principles of food preservation? Explain briefly.

Answer:

  • Food preservation = preventing spoilage by controlling microorganisms, enzymes, and chemical changes.
  • Principles:
    • Removal/inactivation of microbes (heating, sterilization, irradiation).
    • Slowing microbial growth (low temp, chemical preservatives).
    • Preventing contamination (packaging, canning).
  • Aim: Extend shelf life, maintain safety, retain nutrition.

Q2. How does low temperature help in food preservation? Give examples.

Answer:

  • Refrigeration (0â€“5 Â°C): Slows microbial growth and enzyme activity.
  • Chilling: Keeps food fresh for short periods (milk, fruits).
  • Freezing (âˆ’18 Â°C): Stops microbial activity almost completely.
  • Examples: Fresh meat in freezer, milk in fridge, ice cream storage.

Q3. Explain the use of high temperature in food preservation.

Answer:

  • Blanching (90â€“100 Â°C): Destroys enzymes in vegetables.
  • Pasteurization (60â€“80 Â°C): Kills pathogens in milk/juice.

Q4. What are chemical preservatives? Give types and examples.

Answer:

  • Examples: Sodium benzoate in squashes, nitrites in meat.
