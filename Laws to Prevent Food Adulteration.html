Q1. Define food adulteration and explain why laws are needed to prevent it.

Answer:

  • Definition: Food adulteration = mixing, substituting, or removing substances so that food quality, safety, or purity is lowered.
  • Reasons for laws:
    • Protect consumer health.
    • Prevent cheating/fraud in markets.
    • Ensure fair trade practices.
    • Standardize food quality.
  • Exam hook: Without laws, adulteration causes illness, nutrient loss, and loss of trust.

Q2. Write a short history of food adulteration laws in India.

Answer:

  • Ancient times: References in Manusmriti & Sangam texts against impure food.
  • British era: Local rules, municipal bylaws.
  • 1954: Prevention of Food Adulteration (PFA) Act ‚Üí first comprehensive law.
  • 1976‚Äì2000: Supportive acts like Essential Commodities Act, Standards of Weights & Measures.
  • 2006: Food Safety & Standards Act (FSSA) ‚Üí unified all laws, created FSSAI.
  • Today: India follows FSSA 2006 in line with Codex Alimentarius.

Q3. Explain the objectives of laws to prevent food adulteration.

Answer:

  1. Protect public health by banning unsafe food.
  2. Ensure fair trade and stop fraudulent practices.
  3. Set food standards for identity, purity, nutrition.
  4. Provide mandatory labeling for consumer awareness.
  5. Establish inspection and penalties for violators.

Q4. What was the Prevention of Food Adulteration (PFA) Act, 1954?

Answer:

  • First major Indian law against adulteration.
  • Objectives: prevent unsafe/misbranded food sale.
  • Provided:
    • Food inspectors with power to collect samples.
    • Prescribed penalties for violators.
    • Standards for purity and safety.
  • Later repealed and replaced by FSSA 2006.

Q5. Explain the Food Safety and Standards Act (FSSA), 2006.

Answer:

  • Definition: Comprehensive law unifying all food safety regulations.
  • Features:
    • Established FSSAI.
    • Covers food standards, packaging, labeling.
    • Licensing/registration mandatory for food businesses.
    • Penalties and recall system introduced.
  • Importance: Ensures one nation‚Äëone law for food safety.

Q6. List and explain five important provisions of food adulteration laws.

Answer:

  1. Licensing/Registration: Legal approval required for all food businesses.
  2. Standards: Official specifications for purity, additives, contaminants.
  3. Labeling: Name, ingredients, nutrition, veg/non‚Äëveg mark, allergen info.
  4. Inspection & Sampling: Food inspectors test samples regularly.
  5. Penalties: Fines, imprisonment, cancellation of license for violators.

Q7. Discuss the role of FSSAI in enforcing food adulteration laws.

Answer:

  • FSSAI (Food Safety & Standards Authority of India): apex body.
  • Functions:
    • Frame science‚Äëbased standards.
    • License & regulate businesses.
    • Conduct surveillance and inspections.
    • Set labeling and packaging rules.
    • Create awareness among consumers.
  • Works with State Food Safety Commissioners.

Q8. Explain international laws/regulations related to food adulteration.

Answer:

  • Codex Alimentarius (FAO & WHO):
    • Sets global food standards.
    • Ensures fair international trade.
  • FDA (USA): Regulates food safety and additives.
  • EFSA (European Union): Conducts scientific risk assessments.
  • Importance: Provides harmonization, basis for WTO trade disputes.

Q9. What are the penalties for food adulteration under FSSA 2006?

Answer:

  • Penalty for unsafe food: Up to ‚Çπ1 lakh (depending on harm caused).
  • Serious injury: Fine up to ‚Çπ5 lakh + imprisonment up to 6 years.
  • Death: Fine up to ‚Çπ10 lakh + life imprisonment.
  • Minor offences: Misbranding ‚Üí ‚Çπ3 lakh; unhygienic conditions ‚Üí ‚Çπ1 lakh.
  • Exam hook: Penalties act as deterrents against adulteration.

Q10. Discuss the importance of labeling laws in preventing food adulteration.

Answer:

  • Purpose: Provide transparency to consumers.
  • Mandatory info:
    • Food name & ingredients.
    • Net quantity, MRP.
    • Veg/Non‚Äëveg mark.
    • Nutritional info.
    • Allergens.
    • FSSAI license no., batch/lot.
  • Importance: Helps consumers make informed choices and detect misbranding.
