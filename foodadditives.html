1) Introduction

Food additives are substances intentionally added in small amounts to improve safety, shelf life, flavour, colour, texture or nutrition. They may be natural (salt, turmeric, vinegar) or synthetic (aspartame, BHA, tartrazine).

2) Short History

  • Ancient: salt/sugar/vinegar/smoke/spices as preservatives.
  • 19th‚Äì20th c.: chemical preservatives and sweeteners; emulsifiers.
  • Modern: science‚Äëbased regulation (national authorities & Codex).

7) Regulation & Labelling (overview)

  • National authority sets permitted additives and maximum levels for each food category.
  • Labels must declare additives by name or INS code (e.g., Citric acid INS 330, MSG INS 621).
  • Good practice: use the minimum effective level and combine with other hurdles (pH, aw, packaging).
